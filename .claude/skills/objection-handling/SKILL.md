# Objection Handling Skill

Framework for anticipating and addressing prospect objections. Used by the Objection Handler agent.

## The Objection Handling Framework

Every objection has three layers:
1. **What they say** (surface objection)
2. **What they mean** (underlying concern)
3. **What they need** (what would resolve it)

Effective responses address layer 2, not layer 1.

## Common Objection Categories

### 1. Budget Objections
**Surface**: "This is too expensive" / "We don't have the budget"
**Underlying**: "I'm not convinced the ROI justifies the cost" or "I need to justify this to someone else"
**Response pattern**: Reframe cost as investment. Compare to cost of NOT doing it. Break into smaller commitments (starter tier).

### 2. Timing Objections
**Surface**: "Not the right time" / "We're too busy right now"
**Underlying**: "This isn't urgent enough to prioritize" or "I'm overwhelmed and can't take on more"
**Response pattern**: Acknowledge the busyness. Show how you REDUCE their workload, not add to it. Create gentle urgency around opportunity cost.

### 3. Trust Objections
**Surface**: "How do I know you can deliver?" / "We've been burned before"
**Underlying**: "I need proof before I risk my money/reputation"
**Response pattern**: Social proof (case studies, testimonials). Offer a small first step (starter tier). Show process transparency.

### 4. Scope Objections
**Surface**: "Do we really need all this?" / "Can we start smaller?"
**Underlying**: "This feels like more than I signed up for" or "I'm not sure what I actually need"
**Response pattern**: Validate the instinct. Point to the starter tier. Explain why the full package delivers better results. Let them choose.

### 5. DIY Objections
**Surface**: "We can probably handle this ourselves"
**Underlying**: "I think I can save money by doing it in-house" or "I don't see the value over what I'd do"
**Response pattern**: Acknowledge their capability. Highlight the SPECIFIC expertise gap (speed, specialized knowledge, outside perspective). Frame as "you could, but should you?"

### 6. Existing Vendor Objections
**Surface**: "We already work with someone for this"
**Underlying**: "Switching has a cost and I'm not sure you're better"
**Response pattern**: Don't trash the competitor. Acknowledge what works. Focus on the SPECIFIC gap your proposal addresses that the current vendor doesn't.

### 7. Decision Authority Objections
**Surface**: "I need to run this by [boss/partner/board]"
**Underlying**: "I'm interested but can't decide alone" or "I'm using this as a polite delay"
**Response pattern**: Offer to help them make the case. Ask what the decision-maker cares about. Provide a 1-page summary they can forward.

### 8. ROI Objections
**Surface**: "How do I know this will pay off?"
**Underlying**: "I need to quantify the return before I can justify the spend"
**Response pattern**: Provide specific, realistic projections. Use industry benchmarks. Show comparable results from similar clients/projects.

## Response Structure

For each objection, provide:

1. **The objection** (in the prospect's words -- natural, not formal)
2. **Why they think this** (1-2 sentences on the underlying concern)
3. **Your response** (3-5 sentences, conversational):
   - Acknowledge the concern (don't dismiss it)
   - Address the underlying issue (not just the surface)
   - Provide evidence or reframe
   - Offer a path forward
4. **Reframe** (1 sentence that turns the objection into a reason to proceed)

## Response Writing Rules

### DO
- Use natural, conversational language
- Acknowledge the concern genuinely
- Provide specific evidence (numbers, examples, results)
- Offer alternatives (starter tier, phased approach)
- End with a forward-moving question

### DON'T
- Use scripted sales language ("I totally understand your concern...")
- Dismiss the objection ("That's actually not an issue...")
- Be defensive or argumentative
- Make promises you can't keep
- Use pressure tactics or false urgency
- Quote specific pricing (proposals use placeholders)

## Selecting Objections by Prospect Type

| Prospect Type | Most Likely Objections |
|---|---|
| Small business / solopreneur | Budget, DIY, Timing |
| Mid-size company | ROI, Decision Authority, Existing Vendor |
| Enterprise | Scope, Trust, Decision Authority |
| Creator / influencer | Budget, ROI, DIY |
| Startup | Budget, Timing, Scope |
| Agency | Existing Vendor, Trust, Scope |

## Quick Reference Table

Always include a "If They Say → You Say" table at the bottom:

| If they say... | You say... |
|---|---|
| "It's too expensive" | "I hear you. That's why I included a starter tier -- [specific starter benefit]. Most clients start there and upgrade once they see results." |
| "We're too busy" | "That's exactly why this works -- I handle [specific heavy lifting] so your team doesn't have to. The starter package needs about [X hours] of your time total." |
| "We can do this ourselves" | "You absolutely could. The question is whether [specific time/expertise gap] is the best use of your team's time when [specific alternative use of that time]." |
